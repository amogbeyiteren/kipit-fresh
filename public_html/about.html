About Us

At KIPITFRESH, we believe in transforming the journey of fruits from farm to market. Our mission is to redefine 'perishable' by extending the shelf life of fresh produce and reducing waste

About Us

Everyone has a definition of change. To many, it means something different. To us here at KIPITFRESH, change means extended shelf life. Fruits and vegetables deserve to live despite being in the category of "perishables"; we believe there is a fine distinction between perishable and perishing.

Fruit Waste Reduction

The vast amount of fruit waste from the farm to marketplace shows a painful and recurring picture of reduced revenue, environmental pollution, and risky price dynamism, with a total fruit wastage of over 40%.

Building Relationships

We began our journey by building strong relationships with smallholder fruit and vegetable farmers. This involved traveling miles from our operational base into dense thickets within rural communities, speaking local dialects, and investing time to ascertain their volume of produce, storage needs, and level of access to market.

Our Achievements

  • On-boarded the top three major fruit farming communities in the South-South region of Nigeria with a combined total harvest of over 100 tons of fruits weekly.
  • Launched the first-ever electric mobile reefer in Nigeria for smallholder farmers to efficiently store their fruits and vegetables.
  • Launched the first-ever solar-powered refrigeration truck in Africa to facilitate the cold distribution of fruits and vegetables from farm to market.
  • Reached partnership with the top three fruit retailers in Nigeria, placing us among the top 10% of fruit distributors in Nigeria.
  • Commenced FREE training for smallholder fruit and vegetable farmers on the benefits of cold chain as a service.

Our Dedication

Our dedicated team is working assiduously to help reduce post-harvest loss, environmental pollution from fruit waste, reduce carbon emissions from fruit distribution, create access to market, and increase the revenue of smallholder fruit farmers.
